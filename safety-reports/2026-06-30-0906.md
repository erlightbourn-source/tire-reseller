### `2c3a5c1` — Hide moderated/soft-deleted content from public pages (HIGH — PII exposure + moderation bypass)
- `app/sellers/[id]/page.js:33` rendered a seller profile with no `seller.deletedAt` check, leaking a deleted account's **name + location (PII)**; its listings query (line 21) filtered only `status:active`, not `hidden:false`, so listings hidden by account-deletion/moderation still showed.
- `app/listings/[id]/page.js:58` served any listing by direct URL with no `hidden`/`seller.deletedAt` guard for non-owners (and incremented view counts on it) — a scam listing auto-hidden after 5 credible reports, or a deleted seller's listing, stayed fully viewable via its link.
- Every other surface (browse, size/brand pages, alerts cron) already filters `hidden:false` + `seller.deletedAt:null`; this aligns the two detail pages and their `generateMetadata`.

### `9fa8489` — Stop new-match counts from including unviewable listings (MEDIUM — correctness)
- `app/saved/page.js:22` and `app/api/notifications/route.js:38` called `buildListingWhere()` raw (which only adds `status:active`), so saved-search match counts and the notification badge counted hidden listings and listings from soft-deleted sellers — matches the user cannot open (those pages now 404). Added `{ hidden:false }` + `{ seller:{ deletedAt:null } }` to both, matching the browse/alerts contract.

### `14bbce4` — Keep seller ratings in sync after a user purge (MEDIUM — data integrity)
- `app/api/cron/purge/route.js`: hard-deleting soft-deleted accounts cascade-deletes the reviews they authored (`Review.author onDelete: Cascade`, schema line 236), but the denormalized `ratingAvg`/`ratingCount` (User schema lines 29–30) on the reviewed sellers were never recomputed, drifting permanently and feeding the browse `minRating` filter and seller cards stale, inflated numbers. The purge now captures affected sellers before deletion and recomputes their aggregates after the cascade. No schema change.

## Flagged for human review (not changed)

### LOW — Reviews from soft-deleted (not yet purged) authors still count toward seller ratings (data integrity)
- `app/api/reviews/route.js:49-53` aggregates all reviews regardless of author `deletedAt`, and the public seller page (`app/sellers/[id]/page.js:36`) averages all `reviewsReceived`. During the 7-day grace window a deleted reviewer's rating still influences a seller's score; it only self-corrects at purge.
- **Recommended fix:** exclude `author.deletedAt != null` from the rating aggregate and from `reviewsReceived` display.
- **Why a human:** changes review-scoring semantics and ideally pairs with a one-time backfill of existing `ratingAvg`/`ratingCount` — a judgment call beyond a localized bugfix.

### LOW — No automated coverage for page-level visibility guards (resilience)
- The test suite (`test/*.mjs`) is pure-lib only; the hidden/deletedAt guards added in `2c3a5c1`/`9fa8489` aren't exercised by any test.
- **Recommended fix:** add a DB-mocked or e2e test asserting a hidden listing / deleted-seller profile returns 404.
- **Why a human:** requires standing up a new DB-mock/e2e harness (non-trivial, larger than a single locked-in unit test).
